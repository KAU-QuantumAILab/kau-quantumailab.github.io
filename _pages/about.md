**"What is a definition?"**

For artificial intelligence to take its next leap forward, we must overcome a multitude of challenges. The very first step in this process is learning to properly define the problem. Every innovative solution, piece of knowledge, and application ultimately stems from a clear definition.

Because current AI is built upon mathematical and probabilistic models, it faces several inherent limitations. These include significant performance degradation caused by minor perturbations, hallucinations, and the "black-box" phenomenon where the reasoning behind a model's output cannot be explained. To resolve these issues, our lab focuses on the following core research areas:

- **Robust & Trustworthy AI:** We analyze the causes of performance degradation triggered by subtle input variations. By focusing on defense mechanisms against Adversarial Attacks, we aim to proactively secure AI robustness and reliability.

- **Quantum AI:** By combining the overwhelming computational power of quantum computing with artificial intelligence, we are researching a new paradigm designed to maximize the efficiency of existing AI models and break through current computational limits.

We strongly believe that the key to solving all of these technical challenges lies in what was emphasized earlier: problem definition. It is only through in-depth research grounded in rigorous mathematical definitions and solid theoretical foundations that we will finally reach our ultimate goal of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).

Finally, our lab is always open to a wide array of AI-related research topics, even those outside our current core areas. We eagerly look forward to hearing from passionate individuals who wish to explore the fundamental problems of AI and pioneer the future of AGI with us. Please feel free to reach out.
